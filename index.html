Hawaii, the only archipelagic state in the United States, is made up of 132 islands in the central Pacific. The capital is Honolulu on the island of Oahu. The earliest inhabitants were Polynesians, who were followed by European and Asian settlers after 1778.

Located at the "crossroads" of the Pacific Ocean, it is the maritime and air transportation hub between Asia, the United States and Oceania, with an important strategic position

The Kingdom of Hawaii was founded in 1795. It was annexed by the United States in 1898. It belonged to the United States in 1900. It became the 50th state in 1959. Today, the majority of residents are White Europeans, Americans and Japanese, followed by mixed-race people, Filipinos and Chinese.

Hawaii is the only archipelagic state in the United States and is made up of 132 islands in the central Pacific Ocean. The land area is 16,700 square kilometers. Hawaii belongs to the sea island climate, monsoon adjustment all year round, the annual temperature in about 26℃-31℃.
